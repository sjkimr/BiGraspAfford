Collision-Aware Bimanual Grasp Affordance Learning for Dual-Arm Manipulation

Abstract

In complex real-world environments, leveraging both arms becomes essential for efficient robotic manipulation. However, most grasping approaches remain focused on single-arm settings, and directly extending such methods to dual-arm systems leads to ambiguous arm–object assignments, making it unclear which arm is better suited to grasp which region. To address this challenge, we propose BiGraspAfford, a collision-aware bimanual affordance framework that predicts arm-specific grasp affordances from egocentric observations. Our architecture disentangles object-centric representations from scene-level spatial context, enabling the model to reason about potential collisions between the gripper and the environment. We further construct a new bimanual affordance dataset built on the GraspNet-1Billion dataset with arm-specific affordance annotations. Extensive experiments demonstrate consistent improvements over adapted baselines on both seen and unseen objects. We further show real-world applicability by integrating our approach into a hierarchical dual-arm manipulation pipeline.

Contributions

Arm-Specific Affordance

A collision-aware framework that predicts grasp affordances conditioned on object geometry, the executing arm, and surrounding scene context.

Bimanual Dataset

A new dataset extending GraspNet-1Billion with left- and right-arm affordance annotations across cluttered, multi-viewpoint scenes.

Dual-Arm Pipeline

An affordance-guided pipeline integrating perception, vision–language reasoning, and constraint-aware control for robust real-world execution.
